Decidable Problems and Recursive Languages - Undecidability

Throughout my time at university, I have learned about the fundamental concepts of decidability and undecidability in computer science and computational theory. I have come to understand that decidability refers to the ability of an algorithm to determine whether a given input to a problem is true or false within a finite amount of time. Conversely, undecidability refers to problems for which no algorithm exists that can solve the problem for all possible inputs in a finite amount of time. These concepts were first introduced by Kurt GÃ¶del in the 1930s and have been extended to the field of computer science. As I have learned, undecidability limits the extent to which computers can solve certain types of problems, emphasizing the importance of approximation algorithms and heuristics to address these issues.
